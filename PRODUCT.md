# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

French- and English-speaking visitors in Marrakech, especially younger travellers and local diners looking for a rooftop meal in the medina. They usually arrive on mobile while planning the next stop in their day and need to understand the atmosphere, see credible food and terrace photography, then reserve, call, or get directions quickly.

## Product Purpose

Present Simple Restaurant as a distinctive, trustworthy rooftop destination in Riad Laarouss and turn visual interest into table reservations. Success means the site communicates the real sunset-rooftop experience immediately, makes the Moroccan-classics-meets-modern-cafe offer easy to grasp, and keeps the three real conversion paths—reservation, call, and directions—within easy reach.

## Brand Personality

Sunlit, sociable, and assured. The voice is youthful and direct without sounding trendy for its own sake. The desired feeling is the anticipation of arriving on a lively Marrakech rooftop just before sunset.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a generic beige restaurant template, a calm serif-led riad site, a luxury hotel, or a card-heavy AI landing page. Avoid repeated eyebrow headings, equal feature-card grids, excessive pills, invented awards or prices, stock food photography, purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, and ornamental Moroccan clichés. The site should remain clearly distinct from Barouk 155 and recent Essaouira restaurant builds.

## Design Principles

- Let real terrace photography carry the promise of the experience.
- Make the yellow tables and golden-hour light unmistakably Simple.
- Use confident hierarchy and asymmetry to feel lively, not templated.
- Keep every claim honest and every primary action immediately usable.
- Design mobile as the main planning and conversion surface, not a reduced desktop page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard access. Keep visible focus states, semantic landmarks, descriptive image text, and a skip link. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid motion-dependent content, and preserve readable type sizes and touch targets on mobile.
